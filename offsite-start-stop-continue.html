Key Frustrations

  • Airtime was hard to get in the session that mattered most. The AV breakout moved at Dom's pace — fast, tangent-heavy, favoring whoever interjects. Items I cared about didn't make the board. The post-it system (1 per person) compressed what could be expressed.
  • Rare in-person time was spent being talked at. ~80% of the day was presentations. Financial reviews, scorecard explanations, sales strategy — all could have been a pre-read. The opportunity cost of using the one day we're all together for one-directional information transfer is enormous.
  • "This is gonna all start with us." Every operational problem was pushed to the property team to solve first. Eric asked the hardest question of the day — "when does it start going from the top down?" — and the answer was: not yet.
  • Feedback collection has no visible accountability. Whiteboards were photographed. Post-its were placed. Nothing was assigned a timeline, an owner, or a check-in date. If the ETC training doesn't happen, if the switcher doesn't get replaced — next year's offsite will have the same post-its on the same board.
  • The Courtney moment. The SVP of Operations delivered cookies, said "I'm gonna leave you to do things I don't understand," and left. The person accountable for operations at the executive level has no technical understanding of what the AV team does.
  • The Decision Tree asks individuals to solve structural problems. The workshop framed bottlenecks as confidence issues. The AV breakout told a different story: no budget at 311, no planning system, no CATs, no renovation scoping involvement. You can't confidence your way past a missing budget line.
